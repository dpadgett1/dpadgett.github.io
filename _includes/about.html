About Me


I am a philosopher who has found his home in UX design. Sounds strange? It's not as weird as you might think.

  • Through conducting research and teaching I have become adept at information architecture. To teach and write well, you need to understand the systems and structures you're studying, and how to organize that information in a coherent way.
  • User research aims to solicit useful information from your target audience. Ever hear of Socrates?
  • With a rigorous background in critical thinking and logic, philosophers are keen to spot assumptions, dependencies, and contingencies---all crucial elements in crafting an effective experience strategy.
  • Student engagement is a key component of a well-designed lesson. As a teacher and instructional designer, I have become skilled at interaction design, learning what makes for an efficient, effective, and engaging interaction with a product.


Currently I am the LMS Training Lead at the United States Air Force Academy. In this role I focus on helping faculty create learner-centered experiences using the Academy's LMS. I also lead special projects where I design products to help meet the mission of the Academy. If you'd like to see some of the project, please check out my Design Project page.
