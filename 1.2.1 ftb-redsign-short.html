The Challenge

Background: The Faculty Toolbox was created as a resource for faculty to learn about the US Air Force Academy's Learning Management System (LMS). When I started my current role I took over the development of the Toolbox. There were several usability problems that were apparent from day one.

Goal: Redesign and rebrand the Faculty Toolbox to increase usability and traffic to the site.

My Role: Website Designer, User Researcher, Information Architect

The Design Process

User Research

I combined my experience as a faculty member along with informal interviews to create personas for the 5 different types of faculty I discovered during my research.

Common to all personas was a desire for information to be easy to find and the navigation to be “intuitive”. In this case, an “intuitive” navigation is one which maps a three-component mental model faculty have for building a course: course content and delivery, course administration, and assessment.

Results

  1. 46.3% increase in traffic since the redesign, and new users visiting the site every month.
  2. Over 50% of faculty have used the Toolbox and voluntarily adopted Blackboard in under two years ago and despite some very vocal critics.
  3. A consistent user experience across the Faculty Toolbox website.
  4. The implementation of design thinking in LMS-related training at the Academy.
